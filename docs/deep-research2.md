Technical Architecture for Predictive Dispatch in Islanded Microgrids: Integrating LightGBM and Temporal Convolutional Networks for Economic OptimizationThe operational management of islanded electricity grids represents one of the most complex challenges in modern power systems engineering. For islanded grids, maintaining equilibrium between supply and demand is a geographically isolated and resource-constrained task. Traditionally, this has necessitated a heavy dependency on diesel-fired generators to provide dispatchable capacity. However, the inherent volatility of load patterns, combined with increasing variable renewable energy (VRE) penetration, has rendered traditional reactive management strategies economically unsustainable.Predictive dispatch, underpinned by hybrid machine learning (ML) architectures, offers a pathway to solve the primary "Pain Points" of grid operations: high production costs, difficulty in assessing management efficiency, and environmental pollution from diesel reliance.1. This allows the system to assess the "Excess Power" available from mainland links and determine the exact "Delta" that must be covered by local assets.Cost Optimization: Developing a scheduling system that orchestrates diesel generators and BESS to achieve the lowest possible operating cost under current grid constraints. This focuses on finding the mathematical "Break-even Point" for starting a generator versus discharging the battery.Early Warning & Action: Proactively detecting grid stability issues and providing alerts for impending "Bottom neck" events or frequency excursions.
