Technical Architecture for Predictive Dispatch in Islanded Microgrids: Integrating LightGBM and Temporal Convolutional Networks for Economic OptimizationThe operational management of islanded electricity grids represents one of the most complex challenges in modern power systems engineering. Unlike mainland interconnected systems that benefit from the vast rotational inertia and balancing capabilities of continental-scale infrastructures, islanded grids must maintain a precise, millisecond-by-millisecond equilibrium between supply and demand within a geographically isolated and resource-constrained environment. Traditionally, this reliance has necessitated a heavy dependency on diesel-fired internal combustion engine (ICE) generators, which serve as the primary source of dispatchable capacity. However, the inherent volatility of load patterns in small-scale systems, coupled with the increasing penetration of variable renewable energy (VRE) sources such as wind and solar, has rendered traditional reactive management strategies economically and environmentally unsustainable. The emergence of predictive dispatch, underpinned by advanced machine learning (ML) architectures, offers a transformative pathway toward reducing fuel consumption, extending the lifecycle of battery energy storage systems (BESS), and lowering the overall levelized cost of energy (LCOE) in isolated regions.The Socio-Economic and Mechanical Drivers of Grid InefficiencyThe high cost of energy in islanded environments is primarily a function of two factors: the logistical overhead of fossil fuel importation and the technical requirement for "spinning reserve".
